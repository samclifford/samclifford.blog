There is always prior information

  • We never go into an experiment knowing absolutely nothing about our parameters
  • Even if we know next to nothing we can encode it
  • Don’t use stupidly vague priors (we’re all sinners)
  • Priors allow us to do more than just encode stuff from previous studies
    • discussion on regularisation looks a lot like the penalised likelihoods of Eilers and Marx fitting a P-spline
    • Lang, Brezger, et al. showed Bayesian splines use the same approach as P-splines
